Donor Services

Organ and tissue donation saves many lives every year. The majority of donors are deceased, but people can also become a living donor.

More than 100,000 people are waiting for a lifesaving transplant. Transplants rely on the generosity of organ, eye and tissue donors, and there are not enough donors to meet the need. You can help.

Criteria for "Donation"

Are you able to donate?

  • You can donate only if you can more than 18 years. Under 18 or less than 18 aged person are strictly not allow X to donate.
  • You can donate only if you can less than 40 years. Under 40 or more than 40 aged person are strictly not allow X to donate.
  • You can donate only if you don't have any past diease related to organ, if you have any medical diease then you can't donate.
  • Having a serious condition like cancer, HIV, diabetes, kidney disease, or heart disease can prevent you from donating as a living donor.

  • Only medical and logistical factors are used in organ matching. Personal or social characteristics such as status, income or insurance play
    no role in donation.

  • Organs are usually transplanted because the recipient's original organs are damaged and cannot function. The brain is the only organ
    in the human body that cannot be transplanted.
